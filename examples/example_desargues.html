Projective Euclidean Plane Geometry : Desargues Theorem

From wikipedia : For Perspective triangles, corresponding sides of the triangles, when extended, meet at points on a line called the axis of perspectivity. The lines which run through corresponding vertices on the triangles meet at a point called the center of perspectivity. Desargues's theorem states that the truth of the first condition is necessary and sufficient for the truth of the second.

This theorem is 'naturally' dual. The meet of the join of corresponding points is 1 point. The join of the meet of corresponding lines is 1 line.
